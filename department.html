Our Identity

Who We Are

The PG & Research Department of Advanced Zoology and Biotechnology at Loyola College is one of Chennai's most distinguished life sciences departments — blending classical zoology with cutting-edge biotechnology to train world-class scientists, researchers and educators since 1948.

Our Mission

To provide rigorous, research-driven education in zoology and biotechnology — forming critical thinkers, ethical scientists and compassionate citizens who serve society and the natural world with knowledge, integrity and innovation.

Our Vision

To be a nationally recognised Centre of Excellence in Life Sciences — integrating biodiversity conservation, translational biotechnology, and Jesuit values of holistic formation to produce globally competent scientists.

Interdisciplinary Approach

Our curriculum bridges classical animal biology, molecular genetics, microbiology, bioinformatics and industrial biotechnology — preparing students for careers in research, pharma, environmental science, and academia.

Biodiversity & Conservation

Committed to documenting and conserving India's rich biodiversity. Faculty and students conduct field studies in Tamil Nadu's wetlands, forests and urban ecosystems — contributing to national conservation databases.

A Legacy of Discovery

Over 78 Years of Life Science Excellence

From a modest natural sciences unit founded in 1948 to a vibrant PG & Research Department offering UG, PG and Ph.D. programmes — the Department of Advanced Zoology and Biotechnology has grown into one of South India's most respected life science departments.

Our alumni serve as scientists at CSIR, ICMR and DBT institutions, as faculty across Indian universities, and as researchers in biotech industries across the globe.

1948
FOUNDED
The Department of Natural Sciences was started in 1948, laying the foundation for life science education at Loyola College.
1957
ZOOLOGY DEPARTMENT
A full-fledged Department of Zoology was established in 1957, offering B.Sc. Zoology — one of the first in the Madras University system.
1982
PG PROGRAMME
Launch of M.Sc. Zoology, elevating the department to postgraduate status and enabling higher research engagement with the University of Madras.
1998
BIOTECHNOLOGY INTEGRATION
Biotechnology formally integrated into the curriculum — renamed the Department of Advanced Zoology and Biotechnology to reflect its interdisciplinary scope.
2005
RESEARCH CENTRE STATUS
Recognised as a Research Centre by the University of Madras, empowering the department to award Ph.D. degrees in Zoology and Biotechnology.
2024
CENTENARY YEAR
Celebrating alongside Loyola College's centenary — with upgraded laboratory infrastructure, new research collaborations, and the Loyola Research Endowment Fund.

Infrastructure

Laboratories & Facilities

State-of-the-art research and teaching laboratories equipped with modern instruments, supporting high-quality experimental science from undergraduate to doctoral level.

Molecular Biology Lab

Equipped with PCR systems, gel electrophoresis units, spectrophotometers, microcentrifuges and ultra-low temperature freezers for genomics and molecular biology research.

Biotechnology Research Lab

Facilities for cell culture (CO₂ incubator, laminar flow), ELISA, Western blotting, fluorescence microscopy and protein purification — supporting advanced biotech research.

Advanced Microscopy Suite

Compound and stereo microscopes, phase contrast, dark field and fluorescence microscopy. Histology section with microtome, tissue processor and staining facilities.

Bioinformatics Centre

Dedicated computer lab with internet connectivity, bioinformatics software (MEGA, BLAST, CLUSTAL, PyMOL, AutoDock) and access to NCBI, UniProt and PDB databases.

Aquatic Biology Unit

Aquarium facility for maintaining freshwater fish and invertebrate cultures; limnological equipment for field sampling; DO meter, turbidity meter, spectrophotometer.

Entomology Research Institute

A nationally recognised research unit with insect rearing facilities, biopesticide formulation chambers, scanning electron microscope and extensive reference collections.

Departmental Library

Well-stocked with zoology and biotechnology textbooks, journals (Nature, Science, Cell), bound theses, reference specimens and digital access to Elsevier and Springer databases.

Seminar Hall

Dedicated air-conditioned seminar hall with modern audio-visual infrastructure for guest lectures, research presentations, journal clubs and departmental events.
