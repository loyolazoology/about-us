Our Identity

Who We Are

A government-aided Catholic minority institution run by the Society of Jesus — committed to holistic, transformative education for all, regardless of caste, creed or community.

Our Mission

To provide university education in a Christian Jesuit atmosphere of academic rigour — forming men and women for others, particularly from underserved, Dalit and Catholic communities — who lead with conscience and compassion.

Our Vision

To be a premier institution of excellence inspiring innovation, upholding social justice, and creating globally conscious graduates who carry the Jesuit legacy of intellectual depth and moral courage into every walk of life.

Community Outreach

Every UG student completes 120 hours of community service across 50+ urban villages in Chennai. The LRRS initiative aided 1,750+ flood victims. Service to others is woven into every Loyola education.

Jesuit Heritage

Named after Saint Ignatius of Loyola (1491–1556), founder of the Society of Jesus. For a century, the Jesuit principle of cura personalis — care for the whole person — has guided every decision at this institution.

100
A Century of Light

100 Years of Shaping Lives

Loyola College, Chennai, celebrated its grand centenary in 2024–2025 — a hundred years of illuminating minds, transforming lives, and building leaders who serve society with integrity and compassion.

The centenary was inaugurated with a solemn Eucharistic Celebration on 31 July 2024, followed by a civic function graced by Tamil Nadu Chief Minister M.K. Stalin on 1 August 2024.

Administration & Leadership

Our Leadership

Dedicated Jesuit priests and distinguished educators guiding Loyola's century-old mission with wisdom, faith and vision.

St. Ignatius of Loyola
Patron & Founder, SJ

Born in 1491 in the Basque region of Spain, Ignatius founded the Society of Jesus in 1540. His vision of an educated, spiritually formed, and socially engaged human being remains the bedrock of every Jesuit institution worldwide including Loyola, Chennai.

Society of Jesus 1491 – 1556 Canonized 1622
Rev. Francis Bertram, SJ
Founder & First Principal

The visionary French Jesuit priest who founded Loyola College on Sterling Road, Nungambakkam, in July 1925 with just 75 students in three disciplines. His courage and faith laid the cornerstone of an institution that would illuminate Tamil Nadu for a century and beyond.

Principal 1925–1935 French Jesuit
Rev. Dr. Antony Robinson, SJ
Rector

The Rector provides spiritual and administrative oversight of the entire Loyola community, guiding the institution in its Jesuit mission and charting the course during the historic centenary year. He is the spiritual head of the Society of Jesus community at Loyola.

Rev. Dr. D. Thomas Alexander, SJ
Secretary & Correspondent

The Secretary & Correspondent provides visionary leadership and administrative guidance in alignment with the college's Jesuit mission of academic excellence and social transformation.

Rev. Dr. A. Louis Arockiaraj, SJ
Principal

The Principal leads academic excellence across 60+ programmes, fosters innovation and research, and upholds Loyola's century-old commitment to social responsibility. Under his leadership the centenary celebrations were conducted with great grandeur.

Ignatian Pedagogy

Our Core Values

Rooted in the five Jesuit principles that have guided generations of Loyolites to lead purposeful, compassionate lives.

Faith & Justice

Deeply committed to spiritual formation and social justice. Loyola educates students to challenge inequality and advocate for those at the margins of society.

Magis — Excellence

The Jesuit call to always do more — to pursue greater excellence in academics, service, and character. Every Loyolite is challenged to reach beyond the ordinary.

Men & Women for Others

The defining phrase of Jesuit education. Loyola forms graduates who place service to others at the heart of their personal and professional lives.

Cura Personalis

Care for the whole person — intellectual, emotional, spiritual and social. Every student is known, valued and guided in their unique journey of growth.

Global Solidarity

A worldwide network of Jesuit institutions sharing values, research and academic collaboration — Loyola graduates join a truly global community of conscience.

Academic Rigour

Intellectual excellence through innovative pedagogy, interdisciplinary research, and a curriculum that constantly evolves to meet the demands of the modern world.

Service to Society

Transforming Communities

Loyola believes education must serve society. Every student is formed not just as a professional but as a socially responsible human being — through immersive community engagement, grassroots living, and lifelong values of service.
