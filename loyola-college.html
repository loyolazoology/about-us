A Century of Light

100 Years of Shaping Lives

Loyola College, Chennai, celebrated its grand centenary in 2024–2025 — a hundred years of illuminating minds, transforming lives, and building leaders who serve society with integrity and compassion.

The centenary was inaugurated with a solemn Eucharistic Celebration on 31 July 2024, followed by a civic function graced by Tamil Nadu Chief Minister M.K. Stalin on 1 August 2024. The theme: "Expansion, Inclusion & Excellence."

1925
FOUNDED
Founded by Fr. Francis Bertram SJ with 75 students in Mathematics, History and Economics on Sterling Road, Nungambakkam.
1978
AUTONOMOUS STATUS
Among the first eight colleges in India to receive autonomous status from UGC, empowering it to design its own curriculum.
1986
DIAMOND JUBILEE
Diamond Jubilee celebrated with 4,000 students. Continued expansion in courses, research and community outreach.
2024
CENTENARY CELEBRATIONS
100 glorious years. Education conclaves, international conferences, the Loyola Shyam Kothari Centenary Building and the Loyola Research Endowment Fund launched.
Our Identity

Who We Are

A government-aided Catholic minority institution run by the Society of Jesus, committed to holistic, transformative education for all — regardless of caste, creed or community.

Our Mission

To provide University Education in a Christian Jesuit atmosphere of academic rigour, forming men and women for others — particularly from underserved, Dalit, and Catholic communities — who will go on to lead, innovate, and serve society with conscience and compassion.

Our Vision

To be a premier institution of excellence that inspires innovation, upholds social justice, and creates globally conscious graduates who carry the Jesuit legacy of intellectual depth and moral courage into every walk of life.

Community & Outreach

Every UG student completes 120 hours of community service, engaging with 50+ urban villages in Chennai. PG students experience grassroots life, embodying Jesuit values of service. The LRRS initiative aided 1,750+ flood victims in 2015.

Jesuit Heritage

Named after Saint Ignatius of Loyola, born in 1491, who co-founded the Society of Jesus. The Jesuit order has shaped Loyola's identity for a century — rooted in the principle of cura personalis (care for the whole person).

Ignatian Pedagogy

Our Core Values

Rooted in the five Jesuit principles that have guided generations of Loyolites to lead purposeful, compassionate lives.

⚜️

Faith & Justice

Deeply committed to spiritual formation and social justice, Loyola educates students to challenge inequality and advocate for those at the margins of society.

🔥

Magis (Excellence)

The Jesuit call to always do more — to pursue greater excellence in academics, service, and character. Every Loyolite is challenged to reach beyond the ordinary.

🤝

Men & Women for Others

The defining phrase of Jesuit education. Loyola forms graduates who place service to others at the heart of their personal and professional lives.

💡

Cura Personalis

Care for the whole person — intellectual, emotional, spiritual and social. Every student is known, valued and guided in their unique journey of growth.

🌍

Global Solidarity

A worldwide network of Jesuit institutions sharing values, research and academic collaboration — Loyola graduates join a truly global community of conscience.

🏛️

Academic Rigour

Intellectual excellence through innovative pedagogy, interdisciplinary research, and a curriculum that constantly evolves to meet the demands of the modern world.
